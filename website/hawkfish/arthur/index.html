Arthur Thomas Godfrey Wesley

He's here! After 9 long months our baby boy has finally arrived! This page is for family members to keep track of his progress.

News.

26 November 1999

Three months is a long time when you're 14 months old much has happened in Arthur's world since the last website update at the end of the summer.

In a local outdoor pool last September, Arthur took his first "swimming lessons." He spent time splashing, blowing bubbles, going down the water slide, jumping in the pool, and generally having a blast in what felt like a big bath tub with Mom and Dad. Pretty darn cute in his union suit and swim diaper. Towards the end of the class, Arthur surprised us by holding his breath and actually submerging his head for a few seconds.

We stretched out Arthur's 1st birthday celebration over the last week of September and a few weeks in October as well. Thanks to everyone for your generosity. Arthur enjoyed his first taste of cake (especially the way it felt when you squish it) and really liked the balloons, bows, and gift paper. We feel quite touched by everyone's generosity.

Athur has been steadily filling his playdate calendar and has a number of friends who he visits with on a regular basis. Arthur now attends the Infant &Toddler Program twice a week at the Experimental Education Unit at the University of Washington where Paula used to work. He's always very interested in the contents of the sensory table, puzzles, and tools (a budding engineer perhaps?) there, as well as the irresistable stuffed animals that are brought out to play with--especially the stuffed toy duck. He also likes playcourt where they have cool slides and tunnels, as well as art, and snack time, and circle time.

Grandma Sally and some friends from one of Arthur's playgroups came for Thanksgiving Dinner. Nine-month-old Hannah and Arthur had a marvellous time together, including a 5-10 minute across-the-table giggle exchange during dinner. They also spent a lot of time playing with Arthur's toy kitchen and chasing the cats. Arthur loves the kitchen and we just know he's going to make a great chef one day!

We're continually amazed at how much Arthur changes on a daily basis. He can run, jump like a froggie, climb all sorts of things, and can go up and down the stairs independently. He is also learning to feed himself with a spoon , drink from a cup independently and his appetite is growing daily. Mealtimes, as you can imagine, seem to take forever as he collect empirical evidence on everything from what sort of trajectory your mush will have when flung from your bowl across the dining room with your spoon, what happens if you throw all your cheerios, grapes, crackers, string cheese, etc. in your bowl of mush and pour water on it, determining the squish factor and mouth and finger feel of foods, etc. !!

His expressive vocabulary is really growing fast and includes "mum", "dad", "cheese" (accompanied sometimes by the AMSLAN sign), "more," "done,""down" (usually pointing where he wants to go--can also mean "up" "downstairs," "off,""on," etc. ), "bye-bye", "uh-oh" (thanks to Grandpa Tom for teaching him this important phrase), "shoes", "see"/"kitty"? (used for just about anything he is excited by - cats, unnamed foods, airplanes, whatever) and today's new word "outside." His receptive vocabulary is also exploding. Arthur and he clearly uses non-verbal means of communicating (gestures, pointing, grabbing one by the pinkie and dragging one to request information, label, or action when he can't make himself otherwise understood and is quite emphatic and persistent when Mom and Dad don't understood--the nimwits. Most of the time however, Arthur still uses less universal means of communicating. We call it Arthur speak. The intonation, types of sounds he's making, gestures, and facial expressions, all make you think you should be able to understand him if you'd just strain hard enough. We're also having a lot of fun reading books with Arthur. He definitely has his favorites--lately it's Goodnight Gorilla, Pat the Bunny, and books by Sandra Boynton.

27 August 1999

Arthur has turned into quite the globetrotter. At less than one year old he has already been across 10 time zones, from Hawaii in April to the United Kingdom earlier this month. His whirlwind UK tour included stops in Peterborough, Cambridge, Norwich, Higham's Park, Bath, Winchester, Bishop's Waltham, Surbiton and London, and was a great success judging by the ooh-s and aah-s of his adoring relatives. And the day before we all returned he was able to meet Joan Kinstrie, one of his great-grandmothers.

Just in time for the tour, Arthur learned to walk so that it would be easier to spend the ten hour flight running up and down the plane evading Mom and Dad . Fortunately for Mom, Dad and Grandma, the flight over was at night and he slept for most of it, but coming back he met everyone on the 747 several times! Mom and Dad have also learned their lesson from the Los Angeles diaper debacle (say no more) and were prepared for anything, which meant that nothing happened!

Moving back in time, three weeks before the trip to England Arthur went to a wedding on Mom's Dad's side of the family in Atlanta, where he was again greeted enthusiastically by large numbers of cheering relatives. And immediately after returning from Atlanta, the whole family jumped in the car and drove out to Sand Point, Idaho for a family reunion of Mom's Mom's family. Mountains were climbed and Lake Pend Oreille were explored, and he met another great-grandmother, Lois Fisher.

Summer has allowed Mom and Dad to expand Arthur's food repertiore by feeding him out on the deck. This is great for all concerned as any amount of mess can be swiftly dealt with using the pressure washer attachment on the garden hose! Arthur is now eating a wide variety of fruits and vegetables, all of which are fun to squish. And after a hard day of baby activities, Arthur often splashes around in his wading pool, which also cools Dad off after he rides his bike home from work.

23 April 1999

We all spent the week of the 16th on Maui and Arthur decided while Mom and Dad were relaxing that it was time to start working on advanced baby skills, just to help them out. In that one week on vacation he cut two teeth (bottom front incisors) learned to roll over, sit up, crawl and started practicing his "cruising" (walking while holding on). The cruising is still limited, but he can stand and hold on to a chair and is working on transferring his grip to another object (like Dad).

Mom and Dad are now frantically baby-proofing the house. The nursery is mostly OK, but it is amazing how many innocuous looking things are dangerous. Just this morning Dad found that the rubber tip of the doorstop can come off and become a choking hazard. The crib has been lowered all the way and a gate has been attached to the nursery door. Dad's office has also seen the use of power tools and there is now a baby area with an ethernet drop and a gate so that Dad can hang with Arthur and pretend towork occasionally!

This week has also seen the start of solid food. So far Arthur is more interested in playing with food than eating it and has consistently turned up his nose at the rice cereal. On Maui the natives were all extolling the virtues of poi (taro root - the Hawaiian starch staple) and we are looking around for some for him to try.

22 March 1999.

Arthur has been in an infant hearing study at UW Speech and Hearing Sciences in Dr. Lynn Werner's lab.His hearing is testing normal and he enjoyed watching the toy lion and bear light up when he heard a sound. He got a little distracted by the cute baby in the one way mirror and didn't respond to the tones! At any rate, Arthur got his UW graduate T-shirts a lot more quickly than Mom got hers.

This is Arthur's first spring and he is enjoying nature walks with Mom in the fine weather we have been having. Dad takes him out on the dock outside the office and he really seems to like the boats. His favorite game right now is making himself disappear under a diaper or a blanket by covering up his head. Giggling starts when you say "Where did Arthur go?" and play peek-a-boo.

15 March 1999.

Well, it has been a while since the last update and Arthur is growing fast! At five and a half months he is 27 1/2 inches long and weighs in at 15 lbs. 12 oz. Doctor anderson thinks that his eyes will remain Hazel like Dad's. Arthur, Mom and Dad have all been sick recently - Arthur is learning to share.

Arthur's friend Ian LeMaster came over to play on Saturday night while Ian's Mom and Dad went on a date. They had fun together and seemed to enjoy smiling at each other and grabbing each other's faces. Arthur can pretty much sit up by himself now and has a lot to say if only we could understand it. He enjoys sitting up and concentrating on something like toys on an excersaucer or swing. On the mobility front, he is now "air swimming" on his tummy in preparation for crawling. He can also scootch himself around a full 360 degrees on his back or on his tummy.

Arthur is facinated by other creatures, or really anything that moves. People, cats and Dad's twitching feet have all been known to elicit grins and squeals of joy. The cats have learned that he loves to grab their fur and keep just out of reach. He has a lot of fun sitting in his baby carrier on Mom's chest and watching her play this the cats with a "fishing rod" toy.

9 December 1998.

Victory is ours! Grandma and Dad manged to convince young Arthur to take a bottle while Mom was in the basement pretending to have a life. Dad was the underdog going into this contest as he had lost the first round, but with a new bottle and a thick skin he finally persevered. Grandma was along as an observer and finished the feeding for Dad who was having a Gatorade(tm) shower.

7 December 1998.

Today was a traumatic day for both Dad and Arthur as the first bottle feeding was attempted. Preperations went fairly well, but the main event was marred by technical ineptitude on one side and general snarkiness on the other. Very little milk was actually consumed and much of the rest will have to be removed from carpets, clothing etc. before the house starts to smell like a cheese factory. Dad is nursing his wounds and Arthur is sleeping it off. The latter should be well rested for the next round, tentatively scheduled for tomorrow and both sides may wish to have Mom around as a referee.

1 December 1998.

Our son is becoming noisier! Mom the SLP notes that he is expanding his sound inventory beyond "chirping like a cat that has cornered a bug" (a technical term) to include grunts, squeaks, shrieks and wailing. Dad has started referring to him as "the piglet" because of the sounds he makes when eating. In his quieter moments, he likes blowing bubbles, smacking his lips and sticking out his tongue. His hearing is also getting more acute as he learns to turn his head towards new sounds and listen to them, although he still startles at loud sounds. He also likes to make faces with you and imitate your mouth movements if you get up close to his face. He will even smile at you if he is in the mood and his smile is one of pure happiness that is hard to resist!

Arthur is also becoming stronger and more coordinated. When we pick him up, he can hold his head up and when he is underneath his play mobile he can hit Big Bird and make him (her, it?) move. Splashing around in the bath with Mom is also a great source of amusement, and we have started putting his rattly zebra socks on his feet for him to play around with.

Appearance is becoming more human as his hair grows in and wrinkles recede. He still has a bald spot on the back of his head from lying on his back and his eye color is still indeterminate, which leads to Dad buy into the idea that aliens look like children and are really just part of the collective unconcious.

7 November 1998.

Arthur had his one month checkup yesterday and now weighs in at 10.75 pounds! It's amazing how quickly he is growing. Already we are seeing some of his infant clothes looking a little tight and we are about to move up to the next size of diapers from the service. Mom and Dad are getting better at interpreting his moods, which makes everyone happier, and he is starting to sleep for longer stretches of time.

20 October 1998.

Arthur can now track visually to the midline and he is more responsive to his environment, startling to loud unexpected sounds. He has dancing movements with his hands and feet, and can observe faces for 15 minutes (or until dad's arms give out!) He often has a serious or earnest look on his face - is this light sensitivity, or a predisposition to earnestness from one or both of his parents?

19 October 1998.

At his three week checkup, Arthur is doing great, weighing in at 9lbs even and measuring 22 inches long (a whole inch in three weeks!) He has started to have periods where he likes to lie on the floor playing with his arms and legs while his dad reads A.A. Milne to him. He is also eating constantly it seems.

Paula had a bout with mastitis last week but has recovered thanks to an antibiotic regime. Nursing is improving as we all get used to one another.

16 October 1998.

We went to UW Hospital today to show Arthur off at a class for Physical therapists. He started showing tears and a bit of a walking reflex today. In general, he is more alert, his eyes are less crossed, his hair is coming in (including some downy porkchop sideburns). The noise repertoire includes cooing, grunting, smiling and cute little baby snores.

12 October 1998.

Arthur has a belly button now!
