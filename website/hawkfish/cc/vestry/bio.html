Richard Wesley

I have been a parishioner at Christ Church for about ten years. During that time, I have served as a lector and sung in the choir, which is where I met my wife Paula. Several years ago I also spent some time with the Canterbury group, and in recent years I have helped cook for various parish functions. Last winter I gave a talk on my views on Christian Economy to the Adult Education group.

I was born in England and I lived there for four years before my parents emigrated to this country. I spent my childhood in upstate New York. I went to an Episcopal boarding school in New England and studied mathematics in college and graduate school . When I realized that I wasn't cut out to be an academic, I moved to Seattle and joined the information industry. Paula and I were married two and a half years ago at Christ Church and we now live in Wallingford with our son Arthur and our two cats.

For much of my professional life I have been self-employed. At the moment I am an owner and the treasurer of a small contract programming company in Fremont called Electric Fish. We have been in business for about four years and God has been faithful to me in this calling by making our work both enjoyable and moderately successful.

I believe that an important part of the Church's mission is prophetic - "to speak God's Word in the present." If elected to the Vestry I will do my best to facilitate that mission and to be a good steward of the parish gifts.
